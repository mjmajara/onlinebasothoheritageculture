Basotho Traditional Clothing

Basotho Blanket - Worn for warmth and ceremonial occasions, symbolizes heritage.

Mokorotlo Hat - Iconic conical hat made from straw, worn by men and women.

Traditional Dress - Colorful garments representing identity and tradition.

Ceremonial Attire - Worn during special events, weddings, and cultural ceremonies.

Men's Attire - Includes blanket, hat, and accessories for daily and formal wear.

Women's Attire - Traditional dresses, blankets, and headpieces worn proudly.

TS'EA

Tsheha is one of the traditional basotho undergaments, specifically a triangular piece of sheepskin worn by young boys in the past to cover their private parts, which was lengthened to cover more of the legs as the boy grew older. This garment is a significant element of the basotho culture, which boosts a rich heritage of traditional clothing and customs.

SEHOESHOE

This is a distinctive printed cotton fabric deeply rooted in Lesotho. Basotho worn proudly during weddings, initiation ceremonies, and cultural events. It represents dignity, continuity, and identity.

SETSIBA

Setsiba is what the basotho woman wear when they are from the initiation school. It is made from a cow skin.

KHOETSA

This is made up of an animal skin or it can be made of a threat.

KHOTSO

It is made up of seeds for a particular tree, and this dresses infants, it is worn around the waist, to monitor weight and protection against evil.

LEQAPHA

This is a piece of cloth that is worn by the basotho man and it is worn like a diaper it is made from cotton cloth and safety pin. It is worn by shepherds and male initiates.

LITOPO

These are known as gumboots they practically symbolise resilience especially in young boys who herd the cattle in rural places.

MATUTUOANE

This is a special multi-coloured skin blanket. It is made from lion skin or leopard skin and its importance is to inauguration to kingship throne.
